# How to Add a Blog Post - Writing in Microsoft Word

## Overview
You can write your blog posts in Microsoft Word (just like normal!), then easily convert and publish them to the website.

---

## Simple 4-Step Process

### **Step 1: Write in Word**

Write your blog post in Microsoft Word just like you normally would:

- **Use headings** (Heading 1, Heading 2, etc.)
- **Bold and italics** work normally
- **Bullet points and numbered lists** work
- **Keep it simple** - no fancy formatting needed

**Example blog post structure:**
```
[Heading 1] Harvest Season Update

[Normal text] This autumn has been exceptional for our vineyards...

[Heading 2] What Makes This Year Special

[Normal text] Three key factors have contributed:

[Bullet list]
‚Ä¢ Perfect September weather
‚Ä¢ Optimal grape sugar levels
‚Ä¢ Traditional qvevri preparation

[Heading 2] Looking Ahead

[Normal text] We're excited to share...
```

---

### **Step 2: Convert to Markdown**

When you're ready to publish:

1. **Select all your text in Word** (Ctrl+A or Cmd+A)
2. **Copy it** (Ctrl+C or Cmd+C)
3. **Go to this website:** `https://word2md.com/`
4. **Paste your text** in the left box
5. **Click "Convert"** button
6. **Copy the result** from the right box

**That's it!** Your text is now ready for the website.

---

### **Step 3: Add to Website**

1. **Go to GitHub:**
   ```
   https://github.com/gcharris/chateau-alaverdi-site
   ```

2. **Navigate to blog posts:**
   - Click `blog` folder
   - Click `_posts` folder

3. **Create new file:**
   - Click "Add file" ‚Üí "Create new file"

4. **Name your file:**
   - Use format: `YYYY-MM-DD-title.md`
   - Example: `2025-11-15-harvest-update.md`
   - **Important:** Use today's date and end with `.md`

5. **Paste your converted text** into the editor

6. **Save (Commit):**
   - Scroll to bottom
   - Type: "Add blog post about [your topic]"
   - Click "Commit new file"

---

### **Step 4: Check Your Post**

- Wait 2-3 minutes
- Visit: `https://gcharris.github.io/chateau-alaverdi-site/`
- Your post should appear in the blog section!

## Troubleshooting

**Q: The converter changed my text weird**
- A: Try copying from Word and pasting into Notepad first, then copy from Notepad to the converter

**Q: I don't like using the converter**
- A: Just send your Word document to a team member who can post it!

**Q: Can I add images?**
- A: Yes! Save images separately, send to team member to upload

**Q: Do I need to learn Markdown?**
- A: No! The converter does it for you.
